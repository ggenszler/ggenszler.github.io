DEMETER - Marsboreal Greenhouse Design

DEMETER stands for the Deployable, Enclosed Martian Environment for Technology, Eating, and Recreation. I worked on this project for my team design capstone at Dartmouth, which was also an entry to NASA's 2019 BIG Idea Challenge. We were tasked with designing a greenhouse to support a crew of 4 astronauts for a 600 sol mission on Mars.

I led entry, descent, and landing and nutritional studies and co-lead the integrated system modeling. The project centered around the diet and health of the astronauts. To start, I evaluated crops from a database of 90 plants for nutrient requirements and plant growth cycles to serve fresh, nutrient dense foods within 80 sols after landing. Eventually, I arrived at a basket of 8 crops: kale, soybeans, sweet potato, potato, broccoli, strawberries, wheat, and chufa. Throughout the design process, I collaborated with structural lead to reduce payload mass to 9,000 kg based on entry, descent, and landing simulation results. During the later stages of the project, I presented integrated system model results to the power, and environmental leads to lower energy consumption to 20.5 W while maintaining healthy and safe CO2 and O2 levels in the greenhouse.

DEMETER was selected as one of five finalists by NASA in their 2019 BIG Idea Challenge. After presenting at the BIG Idea Forum at NASA Langley, DEMETER was named the winning design. A copy of our final report can be found on the Dartmouth Digital Commons.
